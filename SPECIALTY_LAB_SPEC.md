# Specialty Lab Specification: The BARC Sandbox

**Project**: BPO Architecture Review Copiolet (BARC)  
**Standard**: Knowledge Integration Workbench (KIW)  
**Sub-System**: Specialty Lab (SL-01)  

---

## 1. Concept: The "Golden Standard" Sanctuary
The **Specialty Lab** is a private, high-governance sub-environment within BARC. It serves as the "source of truth" for what constitutes architectural excellence. While the main BARC platform assesses BPO evidence, the Specialty Lab **calibrates** the agents and architects against "Golden Artifacts".

---

## 2. Core Capabilities

### 2.1. Private Artifact Vault (Domain Silos)
- **Security**: Granular RBAC (Role-Based Access Control) ensuring that Network specialists cannot access IT Environment private artifacts unless explicitly granted.
- **Content**: 
    - Proprietary architecture templates.
    - Anonymized "Best-in-Class" configuration logs.
    - Security whitepapers and internal BPO standards.

### 2.2. Golden Standard Benchmarking
- **Function**: Agents use the artifacts in this lab as the baseline for "Ideal State" reasoning.
- **Agent Calibration**: When a Domain Specialist Agent (DSA) encounters new BPO evidence, it first performs a "Similarity Logic" check against the Golden Standard in the Specialty Lab to determine the baseline confidence score.

### 2.3. Historical Context Engine
- **Longitudinal Data**: Stores documentation from previous assessment cycles (years 1-5) to identify trends.
- **Pattern Matching**: "This BPO redundant circuit failure in 2026 matches a pattern identified in the 2023 lab session for a different partner."

### 2.4. Collaborative Simulation (Non-Audited Sandbox)
- **Usage**: Architects can upload "In-Progress" BPO documents to the lab.
- **Agent Interaction**: Thinking Agents provide critiques and suggestions *before* the document is officially submitted to the BARC Audit Trail.
- **Benefit**: Reduces "Audit Friction" by allowing BPOs and Architects to resolve clear gaps in a private, low-stakes environment.

---

## 3. Specialty Lab Workflow (KIW-Grade)

1.  **Artifact Ingestion (Curation)**: Senior Architects curate and upload "Golden Artifacts" to the Lab.
2.  **Context Extraction**: Synthesis Agents index these artifacts, creating an internal "Knowledge Map" of the Golden Standard.
3.  **Simulation Request**: A BPO Worker or Architect initiates a "Lab Session" with a draft document.
4.  **Delta Analysis**: The Lab's specific "Specialty Agent" identifies the delta between the draft and the Golden Standard.
5.  **Refinement**: The Thinking Agent suggests specific improvements to close the delta.
6.  **Transition**: Once refined, the artifact can be "Graduated" to the formal BARC Ingestion pipeline.

---

## 4. Technical Constraints
- **Isolation**: The Lab must use logically separate database schemas or partitioned indices to prevent "Evidence Leakage" into the formal Audit Layer.
- **Immunity**: Actions performed in the Specialty Lab are *excluded* from the formal BPO Trust Score until the artifact is graduated.
- **Encryption**: Artifacts in the vault are encrypted at rest with domain-specific keys.
